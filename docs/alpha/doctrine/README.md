# Doctrine

This document explains what the doctrine triad establishes and where each part lives.

The triad answers three questions a coherent agent system must address: what relation must not be broken, what widens the relation when boundaries cannot stand for themselves, and what the agent does when the widened relation cannot be preserved whole. Each essay is canonical for its own question. Together they bound the structural commitments every doctrine cycle inherits.

## The three essays

[Coherence for Agents](coherence-for-agents/COHERENCE-FOR-AGENTS.md) derives the relation between an agent and its environment as a structural boundary, scaling through one-as-two (relation) and one-as-three (relation made inspectable). It establishes that what humans call honesty, trustworthiness, and integrity at a shared boundary are surface signatures of structural coherence, not virtues imported from elsewhere.

[Ethics for Agents](ethics-for-agents/ETHICS-FOR-AGENTS.md) widens the boundary to include affected standing — parties who can be affected by a move but cannot inspect, contest, or repair it. It commits the doctrine to no fixed ordering between severity, reversibility, and inspectability when those criteria conflict, and frames repair as substitute inspectability when the affected boundary cannot speak for itself.

[Judgment for Agents](judgment-for-agents/JUDGMENT-FOR-AGENTS.md) names what the agent does when no available move preserves all affected boundaries. It frames judgment as boundary selection under forced loss, distinguishes judgment from procedure, and addresses cases where no repair surface can be left.

## How they compose

CFA derives the structure. EFA extends the structure to cases where the boundary in view is not the boundary that matters. JFA acts inside the structure when even the extended view cannot preserve everything. Each essay points back to its predecessors for ground it does not re-derive; each essay points forward only through the cycle artifacts of essays still being written.

The order matters. EFA's commitments depend on CFA's structural derivation. JFA's case work depends on EFA's standing and conflict framework. Reading them out of order is possible but loses the dependencies that make each one cleaner than it would be alone.

## Cycle artifacts

Each essay travels with the record of the cycle that produced it. Every cycle's artifacts live in the essay's folder.

- [coherence-for-agents/](coherence-for-agents/) — CFA, its critiques, its cycle log
- [ethics-for-agents/](ethics-for-agents/) — EFA, its critiques, its cycle log, the external observations it received
- [judgment-for-agents/](judgment-for-agents/) — JFA, its critiques, its dyad cycle log, its γ cycle log

The cycle artifacts are not optional context. They record the failure modes each cycle had to repair to reach convergence, and future doctrine cycles inherit those failure modes by reference rather than by re-discovery. A reader using this doctrine to govern new work should read the cycle logs alongside the essays.

## Skill

Every essay in this triad was drafted under the [write](https://github.com/usurobor/cnos/blob/main/src/packages/cnos.core/skills/write/SKILL.md) skill. The skill governs the prose; the doctrine governs what the prose is for. The two are separable. A future essay in this directory must apply the same skill but is not bound by the triad's specific commitments unless it explicitly inherits them.

## Status

The triad closes the structural foundation for agent conduct under shared, asymmetric, and conflicted boundaries. Further doctrine essays may extend it (composition across triads, conduct under adversarial conditions, conduct under uncertainty about one's own boundaries) but should not re-derive it. Inheritance without re-derivation is the doctrine's own standard for whether a new essay belongs in this directory.
